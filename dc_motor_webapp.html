DC Motor Speed Control

Single-Phase Full-Bridge Converter • 110 V • 5 HP • Kb = 0.055 V/rpm

AC Supply: 120 V rms, 60 Hz

Overview & Theory

Problem Statement

A 110 V, 5 HP DC motor has its speed controlled by a single-phase full-bridge converter. The AC supply is 120 V rms, 60 Hz. Motor voltage constant Kb = 0.055 V/rpm. Armature resistance Ra = 0.5 Ω. Inductance La is high (continuous current assumed).

The full-bridge (H-bridge) rectifier uses 4 thyristors (SCRs) in a bridge configuration. By controlling the firing angle α, the average DC output voltage is adjusted to control motor speed.

Full-Bridge Converter Theory

Supply peak:Vm = Vs × √2
Avg DC voltage:Vdc = (2Vm/π) × cos(α)
Back-EMF:E = Kb × N (V/rpm)
Firing angle:α = arccos(E / Vm2)
Rated current:Ia = P / Vrated
Fund. rms:I1 = 4Ia / (π√2)
Disp. PF:DPF = cos(α)
Distortion F:DF = I1_rms / Is_rms
Total PF:PF = DPF × DF
THD:√(Is² - I1²) / I1 × 100%

Step-by-Step Solution (N = 1000 rpm)

Step 1 — Peak Voltage
Vm = 120 × √2 = 169.71 V
Vm2 = 2×169.71/π = 108.06 V
Step 2 — Rated Current
Ia = 5×746/110 = 33.91 A
P = 5 HP = 3730 W
Step 3 — Back-EMF
E = 0.055 × 1000 = 55 V
Vdc = E = 55 V (at rated)
Step 4 — Firing Angle
α = arccos(55/108.06) = 59.35°
cosα = 0.5090
Step 5 — Thyristor Currents
IT_avg = 16.95 A
IT_rms = 33.91/√2 = 23.99 A
Step 6 — Power Quality
PF = 0.4582, THD = 48.43%
S = 4069 VA, P = 1865 W
